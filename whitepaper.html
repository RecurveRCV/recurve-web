1.0 Abstract

The modern financial landscape, and increasingly the cryptocurrency space, is structured to favor institutional players at the expense of retail investors. Centralized exchanges, venture capital groups, and coordinated market makers construct closed loops of early allocation, asymmetric data access, and market liquidity manipulation, leaving the average individual to bear the downside of market volatility. Recurve (RCV) is introduced as an independent Layer 1 utility and transactional blockchain designed strictly to re-balance this dynamic. Built anonymously and launched without a premine, founder allocation, or venture capital backing, Recurve guarantees mathematical fairness from block zero.

At the heart of Recurve is the BendProof mining system, a deterministic Scrypt-based Proof-of-Work engine designed to distribute 100% of the coin supply directly to early miners using consumer-grade hardware. By scaling difficulty parameters and subsidies across four distinct phases, BendProof protects early distribution and creates long-term protocol scarcity. Furthermore, Recurve introduces the Rage Meter, an automated, real-time mathematical index that aggregates on-chain and global market signals to measure retail market pain and expose institutional manipulation. Recurve is not merely a transactional medium; it is a community-owned technical infrastructure built for the people who have historically been left behind. The curve always bends back.

2.0 The Retail Investor Problem

The primary promise of decentralization has been compromised. The cryptocurrency market has increasingly replicated the gatekeeping and systemic asymmetries of traditional equity markets, resulting in a system where the retail public is utilized primarily as exit liquidity for institutional insiders.

2.1 Market Manipulation Reality

Retail market participants operate under severe informational disadvantages. Market makers and institutional funds routinely employ algorithmic shorting strategies to hunt retail stop-losses, trigger liquidations, and buy back assets at steep discounts. Coordinated whale wallets execute synchronized dumps, leveraging massive capital reserves to cause severe panic in order-books. Furthermore, centralized exchanges have repeatedly demonstrated a systemic bias against their users. During periods of extreme volatility, prominent platforms such as FTX and Binance have frozen transactions, disabled API feeds, or halted withdrawals, leaving retail users locked out of their accounts while institutional clients settle trades through private channels. Additionally, politicians and regulatory insiders with advance knowledge of policy decisions or enforcement actions trade cryptographically-linked assets, locking in risk-free profits before public announcements are made. The retail participant is structurally bound to lose in this insider-first regime.

2.2 The Trust Deficit

Trust in centralized intermediaries has eroded. However, the trust deficit extends to modern decentralized protocols as well. The vast majority of layer-1 blockchains launched since 2020 are characterized by highly centralized ownership structures. Before a single block is mined, developers allocate substantial portions of the token supply to premines, private seed rounds, advisor allocations, and venture capital backers. When these chains list on public markets, early venture investors dump their tokens at multiples of their purchase prices, triggering prolonged downward pressure on public buyers. Centralized founder keys, multisig backdoors, and emergency freeze mechanisms expose "decentralized" projects as corporate entities subject to regulatory capture or developer whim. The retail public is forced to buy into a rigged cycle that repeats every bull run.

2.3 What Has Been Missing

A Layer 1 protocol built specifically to exclude these asymmetries has been absent. The market lacks a chain that is built for retail, by retail, with mathematical rules that prevent the introduction of early privilege. To fix this trust deficit, a protocol must contain: zero premine, zero private venture allocations, zero founder allocations, and zero administrative backdoors. Furthermore, the community requires open-source mathematical tools to measure and expose manipulation in real time, shifting the balance of power from hidden exchange boardrooms to public, auditable blockchains.

3.0 Introducing Recurve

Recurve represents a return to the foundational principles of peer-to-peer digital cash, augmented with consensus-level features designed to support retail distribution and expose market manipulation.

3.1 What Recurve Is

Recurve (RCV) is an independent, Layer 1 blockchain developed to prioritize open-access mining and retail utility. The protocol takes its name from the recurve bow—a structure designed to store maximum tension when pulled, only to release that energy with velocity. This metaphor represents the retail market: though pulled back and compressed by institutional forces, the curve always bends back. Launched under a pseudonym to prevent regulatory pressure or centralized coordination, Recurve operates as a pure public utility.

3.2 Core Principles

The protocol enforces five core architectural and economic commitments:

  • NO PREMINE: The genesis block contains zero minted coins. Every RCV in existence must be earned via public CPU/GPU mining under the BendProof algorithm.
  • NO VC FUNDING: No seed rounds, private allocations, or pre-sales were conducted. Institutional capital has no early access to the supply.
  • NO FOUNDER KEYS: There are no developer backdoors, master nodes with special privileges, or protocol-upgrade keys. The network relies strictly on decentralized consensus.
  • NO INFLATION: The total coin supply is capped at exactly 10,000,000,000 RCV. Once the final block reward is claimed, no new coins can ever be created.
  • NO CENTRAL POINT OF FAILURE: The node software is designed to run on consumer hardware, encouraging a geographically distributed network of validation nodes.

3.3 The Recurve Rage Meter

To provide the retail community with real-time insight into market manipulation, the project integrates the Recurve Rage Meter. The Rage Meter is a publicly accessible dashboard that tracks market distress by calculating four weighted variables:

  1. Market Pain Score (40%): An index tracking the percentage of negative top-100 coins and their average 24-hour loss percentage.
  2. Inverted Fear & Greed (20%): An index that translates high market fear into an indicator of retail capitulation.
  3. BTC Dominance Pressure (20%): A pressure index tracking Altcoin bleeding as capital flies back to Bitcoin.
  4. Market Crash Velocity (20%): A measure of sudden, rapid drops in total market cap over a 24-hour window.

This score is calculated client-side and displayed transparently to expose market dumps and manipulation as they occur.

4.0 BendProof: The Technical Foundation

The Recurve network is built to prioritize technical accessibility, censorship resistance, and cryptographic stability.

4.1 Blockchain Overview

Recurve utilizes a UTXO transaction model, providing robust security, deterministic transaction verification, and parallel validation capabilities. The target block spacing is 60 seconds, which minimizes confirmation latency while preventing chain reorganization overhead. The difficulty adjustment algorithm uses a DigiShield retargeting calculation, adjusting the target difficulty every 60 blocks (approximately 1 hour) to react dynamically to changes in mining hash rate.

4.2 BendProof Mining Algorithm

BendProof is a custom-calibrated, memory-hard proof-of-work algorithm based on Scrypt. The algorithm is designed to remain memory-intensive, preventing early ASIC dominance and allowing consumer CPU and GPU hardware to compete effectively during the initial distribution phases.

Additionally, BendProof implements a deterministic block subsidy schedule based on height, ensuring that the hardest phase limits are unchangeable. Difficulty limits scale across the phases to adjust the proof-of-work target dynamically relative to total coins minted. This design guarantees that as the network matures and industrial mining enters the system, early participants have already secured a significant percentage of the total supply at lower hash-power requirements.

Miners use the open-source cpuminer-multi tool (github.com/tpruvot/cpuminer-multi) configured to connect to their local recurved node via RPC. No proprietary mining software required.

4.3 Network Architecture

The main network uses dedicated parameters to prevent overlap with existing blockchains:

  • P2P Port: 9444 (facilitating node-to-node communications)
  • RPC Port: 9445 (handling API and client queries)
  • Network Magic Bytes: `0x52 0x43 0x56 0x31` ("RCV1" in ASCII)
  • Address Prefix: Base58 decimal 60 (generating addresses starting with "R")
  • Spines: Peer-to-peer full validation nodes
  • Strikes: Cryptographic blocks mined on the chain
  • Benders: Network miners running the BendProof algorithm

4.4 Security Model

Recurve's security relies on the distribution of its validating nodes ("Spines"). By utilizing Scrypt-based mining that remains consumer-accessible during Phase 1, the protocol prevents hash power from consolidating in centralized server farms. A 51% attack on Recurve is prevented by the growing economic cost of acquiring decentralized CPU and GPU hash power across thousands of individual home miners. The open-source code base is public, and the absence of developer administrative keys means the blockchain cannot be frozen or updated without a consensus supermajority of the active nodes.

5.0 Supply Architecture

The economic design of Recurve ensures maximum early incentives for the retail community, followed by a sharp block reward tapering to enforce long-term scarcity.

5.1 Total Supply

The maximum lifetime supply is hard-capped at 10,000,000,000 RCV (10 Billion). There is no premine, meaning that at genesis, the active circulating supply is exactly 0. Coins can only be introduced into circulation via block rewards earned by miners.

5.2 The Four Phases

The BendProof reward schedule is divided into four distinct phases designed to align incentives with network maturity:

Phase 1: "The Awakening" (Strikes 0 to 400,000)
During this phase, each block provides a reward of 10,000 RCV. A total of 4,000,000,000 RCV is minted. The algorithm remains highly accessible, allowing standard CPUs and laptop hardware to participate. The objective is to distribute the initial supply as widely as possible to the retail public.

Phase 2: "The Resistance" (Strikes 400,001 to 1,000,000)
The reward is reduced to 5,000 RCV per block, minting an additional 3,000,000,000 RCV. As network hash rate grows, consumer-grade GPUs are recommended to remain competitive. This phase marks the growth of the node validator ecosystem.

Phase 3: "The Reckoning" (Strikes 1,000,001 to 3,000,000)
The reward drops to 1,000 RCV per block, releasing 2,000,000,000 RCV into circulation. Mining difficulty increases, and dedicated mining rigs become active. Scarcity parameters begin to impact circulating supply dynamics.

Phase 4: "The Vault" (Strikes 3,000,001 to 13,000,000)
The final phase provides a block reward of 100 RCV, minting the final 1,000,000,000 RCV over a period of approximately 19 years. Mining requires industrial hardware. The low emission rate enforces extreme protocol scarcity. Once Strike 13,000,000 is reached, the block reward drops to 0 RCV forever.

5.4 Why This Model

Unlike premined tokens where insiders dump on the market post-launch, Recurve's progressive emission ensures that only active network participants receive coins. In traditional block halving schedules, early emission is often too scarce to incentivize wide retail adoption, whereas Recurve concentrates 70% of the lifetime supply in the first two phases. This model prioritizes the community, rewarding those who believe in the protocol first.

6.0 Beyond the Coin

Recurve is designed to support a practical, censorship-resistant digital ecosystem that extends beyond speculative trading.

6.1 Recurve Pay

Recurve Pay is a proposed standard for merchants wishing to accept RCV payments. By utilizing the 60-second block confirmation window, Recurve Pay allows merchants to settle transactions at negligible network fees, circumventing traditional credit card networks and centralized merchant acquirers.

6.2 Recurve DAO

Ecosystem governance will be coordinated through the Recurve DAO. Node operators ("Spines") and miners ("Benders") can draft and vote on core protocol adjustments. The network relies on a strict proof-of-node mechanism for voting, preventing capital centralization from overriding community intent.

6.3 Recurve Market

The Recurve Market is a proposed decentralized trading protocol built on top of the Layer 1 chain. The market will support atomic swaps and peer-to-peer order book matching, ensuring that RCV and its tokens can be traded securely without KYC, counterparty risk, or administrative halts.

6.4 The Rage Meter

The Rage Meter serves as the public diagnostic tool for the ecosystem. It is accessible globally without subscription fees, providing users with a quantitative representation of retail market strain. This tool is designed to help the community recognize and avoid institutional liquidation traps.
